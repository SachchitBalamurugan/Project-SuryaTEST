ABOUT US

Project Surya

A Bengaluru based 1M1B Future Leader project

Project Surya is a Bengaluru based 1M1B Future Leader project started by a 13-year-old Future Leader, Divaa Uthkarsha.

At Project Surya, we believe that everyone deserves equal opportunities. But that is absent in the case of the underprivileged. If the underprivileged are out of focus then, what happens to the diabetic underprivileged? Moreover, the underprivileged Type-1 diabetic adolescents. As a 1M1B Future Leader project, we will go above and beyond to provide these diabetics the best of care they deserve.

From support groups to family counselling, we aim to sweeten as many lives as possible.

Who Are We

Project Surya is an internationally recognized, entirely youth-led non-profit based in India but present globally, aiming to empower T1 diabetic adolescents from underprivileged and remote communities. We have a three-pronged action plan that covers awareness, advocacy and funding

Vision, Mission and Goal

We envision a world where good health and well-being are made accessible, affordable and available to everyone, including millions of T1 diabetics globally. Our mission is to ensure that every child with T1 diabetes achieves their best state of health through positive aid and timely intervention. Our goal is to make quality insulin and diabetes management resources like strips and syringes accessible to people of all social and financial backgrounds and to ensure that accurate knowledge and awareness are made easily available along with diabetes-friendly policies at the advocacy level.

Our Story

Started by our founder and CEO Divaa Uthkarsha in 2021, Project Surya aims to make good health and well-being accessible, affordable and available to everyone. Divaa’s younger brother Surya was diagnosed with T1 diabetes, and after observing first-hand how challenging the process could be - from accepting the shocking diagnosis, processing that you will have to live with it for the rest of your life, thinking about the added financial constraints and burden on the family and also societal taboos, misconceptions, lack of knowledge and taunts - receiving a T1 diabetes diagnosis is hard. This got to Divaa, she started thinking about the less-privileged kids who had the same condition as her brother. By seeing firsthand the work, money, and family support needed to effectively manage the disease, Divaa wondered what it must be like for underprivileged children suffering from T1D. She realized how unfair the world can be. How can these low-income uneducated families afford to spend more than 70% of their income on out-of-pocket expenditure for diabetes?

Overcome with a sudden motivation, Divaa made a decision, she would do whatever she could to make sure that all children with T1 diabetes can dream big, just like any other child. Hence she started Project Surya in Bangalore, India- in the name of her younger brother. “Surya” in the Kannada language means the Sun, and we want to brighten as many lives just like the Sun does. We are now led by young people from all over the world from 13 cities and 5 countries.
